# Florida-Specific Medicaid Therapy Utilization

-------------------------------------------------------
Using Florida AHCA Medicaid claims data, find any published reports showing:

- Total number of children receiving outpatient PT, OT, or speech therapy under Florida Medicaid by diagnosis category (autism/ASD vs non-ASD developmental delays)
- Average number of therapy visits per year per child for Florida Medicaid enrollees with autism (ICD-10 F84.x)
- Total Florida Medicaid expenditures on outpatient PT, OT, and speech therapy for children with ASD
- Any Florida-specific data from AHCA annual reports, legislative budget requests, or Medicaid managed care plan reports (Sunshine Health, Molina, etc.)

I'm specifically looking for Florida data, not national. Check AHCA.myflorida.com, Florida Senate/House appropriations reports, and Florida Medicaid managed care plan quality reports.

Florida Medicaid has published very little diagnosis‑specific therapy utilization for children; the one place where AHCA did a claims‑based breakdown is the 2013 “Autism / Down Syndrome” report prepared under s.409.906(26), using SFY 2010–2011 FMMIS claims. That report, plus a few policy documents and managed‑care program descriptions, are essentially all that exist publicly.

Below is what can be supported directly from those sources, and where there are gaps.

***

## 1. Total number of children receiving PT/OT/SLP by diagnosis (Florida Medicaid)

The **key Florida‑specific, claims‑based source** is:

- AHCA, “Report and Recommendations for Implementation of 409.906(26), F.S.” (Feb 8, 2013), often referred to as the **Autism Report**. It explicitly states that it uses **SFY 2010–2011 Medicaid claims data from FMMIS/DSS**.[^1]

From that report:

- Estimated Medicaid‑enrolled children (0–20) with **ASD or Down syndrome** (based on prevalence, SFY 2011–12):
    - Total Medicaid‑enrolled children 0–20: 2,133,600
    - Estimated with ASD: 24,245
    - Estimated with Down syndrome: 3,088
    - Total with ASD or Down syndrome: **27,333**[^1]
- Actual **Medicaid State Plan PT/OT/ST caseload, children with ASD and/or Down syndrome, SFY 2010–11** (average monthly caseload from claims):[^1]
    - Ages 0–5: **2,045** children
    - Ages 6–20: **3,073** children
    - Total 0–20 receiving State Plan PT/OT/ST: **5,118** children, all with ASD and/or Down syndrome.

Breakdown by diagnosis for **children actually receiving PT/OT/ST** (average monthly caseload, SFY 2010–11):[^1]


| Diagnosis group | Ages 0–5: avg monthly PT/OT/ST caseload | Ages 6–20: avg monthly PT/OT/ST caseload |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| ASD only | 1,013 | 2,287 |
| Down syndrome only | 892 | 569 |
| Both ASD and Down syndrome | 140 | 217 |

So, using Florida AHCA Medicaid claims:

- **Children with ASD (alone)** receiving PT/OT/ST under the State Plan (average month, SFY 2010–11):
    - 1,013 (ages 0–5) + 2,287 (ages 6–20) ≈ **3,300** children.[^1]
- **All children with ASD and/or Down syndrome** receiving PT/OT/ST: 2,045 (0–5) + 3,073 (6–20) = **5,118**.[^1]

Limitations relative to your ask:

- This report **groups only ASD and Down syndrome**. It does **not** separately identify “non‑ASD developmental delays” (e.g., F80.x speech delay, other developmental disorders) receiving PT/OT/SLP.
- PT, OT, and speech are **aggregated**; there is no separate count by discipline.

There are no newer AHCA publications that repeat this kind of diagnosis‑specific therapy caseload table for other developmental delay categories.

***

## 2. Average number of PT/OT/SLP visits per year per child with autism (Florida Medicaid)

The same Autism Report provides **cost per child**, but **not visit counts or units**.

From the PT/OT/ST cost table (SFY 2010–11 Medicaid State Plan PT, OT, ST):[^1]

**Average annual cost of PT/OT/ST per child** with ASD (State Plan only):

- Ages 0–5, ASD only: **3,695 dollars per child per year**
- Ages 6–20, ASD only: **4,039 dollars per child per year**[^1]

(Values for Down syndrome and ASD+Down are similar but slightly different.)

However:

- The table does **not** report **average number of visits** or hours per child.
- AHCA notes that these costs are based on therapy fee schedules and utilization, but the underlying claim‑level visit data are not presented.[^1]

Because the report only publishes **dollars per child**, not units, any “average visits/year” would have to be inferred from the therapy fee schedule (e.g., dividing 3,695 dollars by a typical reimbursement per visit). That would be **a modeled estimate, not published Florida data**.

No other AHCA or Florida legislative report was found that directly reports:

- “Average number of PT/OT/SLP visits per year” for **children with autism (F84.x)** under Florida Medicaid.

So on this point, **there is no published Florida Medicaid number**; you would need direct access to FMMIS claims or the therapy UM vendor’s data.

***

## 3. Total Florida Medicaid expenditures on PT/OT/SLP for children with ASD

The Autism Report again is the only place where AHCA published a clear dollar figure tied to claims:[^1]

> “Medicaid pays approximately **19.5 million dollars per year** for Medicaid State Plan occupational, physical and speech therapies for an average monthly caseload of **5,118 children** (2,045 children ages 0–5 and 3,073 children ages 6–20), 20 years of age and under **diagnosed with ASD or Down syndrome or both**.”[^1]

Key points:

- 19.5M dollars/year is **total State Plan PT/OT/ST expenditures** for children **with ASD and/or Down syndrome** (not ASD alone), ages 0–20, based on SFY 2010–11 claims.
- The report does **not** break that 19.5M down into:
    - PT vs OT vs speech components, or
    - ASD vs Down syndrome shares.

It does provide the **per‑child average PT/OT/ST cost** for each diagnosis group (ASD, DS, ASD+DS) and age band (0–5, 6–20):[^1]


| Diagnosis group | Ages 0–5: avg annual PT/OT/ST cost per child | Ages 6–20: avg annual PT/OT/ST cost per child |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| ASD only | 3,695 dollars | 4,039 dollars |
| Down syndrome | 3,297 dollars | 3,718 dollars |
| ASD + DS | 3,394 dollars | 4,461 dollars |

Because AHCA did not publish the **ASD‑only subtotal** of the 19.5M dollars, any ASD‑specific expenditure total would have to be reconstructed by combining these per‑child figures with the average monthly caseload counts. That would again be a derived estimate, not an official published number.

No later AHCA budget or quality‑strategy documents were found that update this ASD‑specific PT/OT/SLP spending figure.

***

## 4. Other Florida‑specific AHCA or plan reports using Medicaid claims

A few additional Florida sources are worth noting, even though they do **not** provide the exact PT/OT/SLP utilization metrics you requested:

### a) AHCA Behavior Analysis (BA) coverage policy and information

- **Florida Medicaid Behavior Analysis Services Coverage Policy** (Rule 59G‑4.125 draft and related documents) explicitly states that Florida Medicaid **covers up to 40 hours per week of BA intervention services** for recipients under 21, when medically necessary, as indicated in a prior‑authorized behavior plan.[^2]
- The policy is based on FMMIS claims experience and Garrido v. Dudek litigation, but it **does not** publish visit/hours per child or cost totals; it just sets coverage limits and clinical criteria.[^2]

AHCA also hosts an online **Behavior Analysis Profile** interactive report (Tableau dashboard) for BA services, described as reporting BA service demographics, utilization, and provider participation. However:[^3]

- The dashboard is interactive and not captured in static PDF form.
- The data appear to be **aggregated for ABA/BA only**, not PT/OT/SLP.
- It does not, in any publicly downloadable form, give you the PT/OT/SLP metrics.


### b) KidCare / Medicaid quality evaluations (UF ICHP reports)

The Florida KidCare Program Evaluation reports (SFY 2020–21 and 2021–22) commissioned by AHCA and the Florida Healthy Kids Corporation contain extensive **HEDIS and utilization metrics** (well‑child visits, ED use, behavioral health follow‑up, etc.) for Medicaid Managed Medical Assistance (MMA), MediKids, and CMS Plan enrollees. But:[^4][^5]

- They focus on **quality measures**, not therapy‑specific claims volume.
- They **do not** break out PT/OT/SLP utilization or cost by diagnosis (e.g., ASD vs F80.x).


### c) Florida Medicaid therapy services \& UM program

The AHCA **Therapy Services** page describes the Medicaid therapy program (PT, OT, SLP, RT) and notes a **utilization management program** mandated by the Legislature in s.409.912(40), F.S., intended to ensure medically appropriate therapy use.[^6]

- The page references a **Therapy Services Coverage and Limitations Handbook** and UM legislation but does **not** include any aggregated utilization statistics.
- No public UM evaluation or annual report was found that breaks down **PT/OT/SLP visits or spending by diagnosis** (ASD vs other developmental delays).


### d) Florida Medicaid managed care plan materials

Sunshine Health (Florida Medicaid MMA / Child Welfare) and other plans publish:

- Clinical policies for **Behavior Analysis** (ABA) that mirror Florida Medicaid’s 40‑hour/week limit and define **focused (10–25 hours/week) vs comprehensive (30–40 hours/week) ABA** intensity.[^7]
- A now‑retired Sunshine Health **Medicaid BA Value‑Based Program** which set **age‑based “evidence‑based hourly benchmarks”** for children with ASD and other IDDs:[^8]
    - <4 years: 30–40 hours/week
    - 4–6 years: 20–30 hours/week
    - 7–12 years: 10–15 hours/week
    - 13+: 5–10 hours/week

These documents clearly rely on **Florida Medicaid claims** to define benchmarks and measure compliance, but they **do not publish**:

- Counts of children with ASD by therapy type.
- PT/OT/SLP visit frequency or expenditures by diagnosis.

Managed‑care quality reports (e.g., Sunshine’s QI program summaries, Molina reports) focus on HEDIS‑style measures and generally **do not break out therapy utilization by ICD‑10 diagnosis**.

***

## 5. What Florida Medicaid data you can and cannot get publicly

Based strictly on **published AHCA and Florida plan documents**:

You **can** get:

- **Counts of Medicaid‑enrolled children with ASD and/or Down syndrome** and estimated prevalence in Florida (circa 2010–2012).[^1]
- **Counts of children with ASD and/or Down syndrome who actually received State Plan PT/OT/SLP services**, broken down by age (0–5 vs 6–20) and diagnosis group (ASD only, Down only, ASD+DS), from SFY 2010–11 FMMIS claims.[^1]
- **Per‑child average annual cost** of State Plan PT/OT/SLP for these diagnosis groups, from the same claims.[^1]
- **Total annual Medicaid State Plan PT/OT/SLP spending (~19.5M dollars) for children with ASD and/or Down syndrome** (ages 0–20) in SFY 2010–11.[^1]
- **Clinical and coverage parameters** for ABA/BA in Florida Medicaid (up‑to‑40 hours/week, medical necessity criteria) and managed‑care benchmarks for ABA service hours, but not actual utilization statistics.[^9][^8][^7][^2]

You **cannot** get, from publicly available Florida AHCA or plan reports:

- **Average number of PT, OT, or speech visits per year per child** with **autism (F84.x)** in Florida Medicaid.
- A **Florida‑specific breakdown of PT vs OT vs SLP** utilization or expenditures within that 19.5M dollars.
- Therapy utilization **for non‑ASD developmental delays** (e.g., F80.x speech and language disorders, global developmental delay, etc.) comparable to the ASD numbers.
- Recent (post‑2010‑11) updates of ASD‑specific PT/OT/SLP utilization – the Autism Report appears to be a one‑off analysis done to support 409.906(26), and nothing similar has been republished since.

To get exactly what you asked for (e.g., visits per year per child for Florida Medicaid enrollees with F84.x vs F80.x in PT/OT/SLP), you would need:

- **Direct access to FMMIS claims** or
- Access to the **therapy UM contractor’s data warehouse** or
- A formal data‑use agreement with AHCA, then run custom analyses.

The 2013 Autism Report is the only document that comes close to what you want and is clearly labeled as based on Florida Medicaid claims. Everything beyond that (visits per year, ASD vs other DD for therapy specifically) does not exist in published form.
