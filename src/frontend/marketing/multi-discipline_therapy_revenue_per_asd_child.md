# Many children with autism receive therapy across multiple disciplines simultaneously (e.g., ABA + OT + speech, or PT + OT + speech). What data exists on:

- Average number of therapy disciplines a child with ASD receives concurrently
- Total combined therapy spending across all disciplines per ASD child per year
- How often ASD children receive services from 2+ disciplines at the same provider/clinic vs different providers
- Revenue data from multi-disciplinary pediatric therapy practices that serve primarily ASD populations
- Any data from CentralReach, Rethink, or other ASD-focused practice management platforms on cross-discipline utilization

I want claims-based or survey-based data, not estimates. Check CMS datasets, MEPS data, Medicaid utilization reports, and industry analyses.

There is essentially **no claims‑based dataset in the public domain** that answers your questions in the exact way you framed them (e.g., “average number of therapy disciplines concurrently” or “% receiving 2+ disciplines in one clinic vs multiple clinics”). What exists are:

- Claims and survey studies that report **per‑child service use and costs by category** (speech, OT/PT, behavior therapy, etc.).
- System‑level expenditure estimates (e.g., MEPS).
- Vendor market reports (CentralReach) that show **growth of multidisciplinary care**, but not per‑child discipline counts.

Below is what can be supported with real data, and where the gaps are.

***

## 1. Average number of therapy disciplines a child with ASD receives concurrently

No large U.S. claims database or national survey reports a statistic like:

> “On average, children with ASD receive X therapy disciplines at the same time.”

What we do have are:

### a) Per‑child visit counts by therapy category (Medicaid vs private)

Wang et al. analyzed 2003 Medicaid and private insurance claims for 18,108 Medicaid and 2,366 privately insured children with ASD across 24 states. They reported **mean annual visits per ASD child** for specific outpatient therapy categories:[^1]

- **Medicaid‑enrolled children with ASD (per child per year):**
    - Speech therapy: **13.0 visits**
    - OT/PT (combined): **6.4 visits**
    - Behavior modification/social skills: **3.8 visits**[^1]
- **Privately insured children with ASD (per child per year):**
    - Speech therapy: **3.6 visits**
    - OT/PT: **0.9 visits**
    - Behavior modification/social skills: **1.1 visits**[^1]

These are **averages across all ASD enrollees** in each insurance group, not just “users” of those services. They clearly show that a large proportion of children are receiving **multiple therapy categories**, but the paper does **not** report:

- What share receive **speech + OT/PT**,
- Or **speech + OT/PT + behavior mod** concurrently,
- Or how many disciplines they have **at the same time**.

So concurrency must be inferred qualitatively: the fact that mean visits are non‑trivial across multiple categories implies that a substantial fraction of ASD children are in **two or more therapy types** in a given year—but the exact distribution is not published.

### b) Service‑type counts over time (number of services, not disciplines)

The Canadian national needs assessment (CASDA) found that autistic individuals typically use **several different services over the lifespan**:

- Preschool children: mean **5.8 services** used
- School‑aged children: **7.8 services**
- Adults: **8.9 services**[^2][^3]

These “services” include therapy, education, respite, etc., not just clinical disciplines, and they are cumulative across time, not strictly concurrent.

### Bottom line on “average number of disciplines”

- Claims and survey data show **broad use of multiple therapy categories** (speech, OT/PT, behavior therapy) among ASD children.[^2][^1]
- **No study** was found that specifically reports the **average number of therapy disciplines a child is receiving concurrently** (e.g., ABA + OT + SLP at once) in a claims‑based way.

***

## 2. Total combined therapy spending per ASD child per year

Here there are some solid system‑level and claims‑based numbers, but not partitioned exactly the way you might want (e.g., separate ABA vs OT vs SLP broken out and summed).

### a) National all‑service expenditures (MEPS)

The 2025 AHRQ MEPS Statistical Brief \#565 reports for U.S. children 3–17:[^4]

- Children with **treated ASD**: mean **20,122 dollars/year** total health expenditures.
- Children without treated ASD: **2,201 dollars/year**.[^4]

By service category for ASD children:

- **Ambulatory care** (office‑based + outpatient + ED): **10,279 dollars/year** (51% of total).
- **Home health care**: **7,168 dollars/year** (35.6% of total).[^4]

Ambulatory care for ASD children includes **all outpatient visits**, such as behavioral therapy, OT/PT, and speech, but MEPS does **not** break out those disciplines separately.

### b) Detailed therapy categories in claims (Medicaid vs private)

Wang et al. (claims, 2003) provide per‑child annual **costs for specific outpatient procedures** for ASD children:[^1]

- **Medicaid‑enrolled ASD children (per child per year):**
    - OT/PT: **6.4 visits**, **271 dollars**
    - Speech therapy: **13.0 visits**, **569 dollars**
    - Behavior modification/social skills: **3.8 visits**, **440 dollars**[^1]
- **Privately insured ASD children:**
    - OT/PT: **0.9 visits**, **45 dollars**
    - Speech therapy: **3.6 visits**, **227 dollars**
    - Behavior modification/social skills: **1.1 visits**, **67 dollars**[^1]

Total health costs per ASD child:

- Medicaid: **22,653 dollars/year**
- Private insurance: **5,254 dollars/year**[^1]

Those therapy categories are only a **portion** of total outpatient costs and **exclude intensive ABA/EIBI billed under other codes**; they are also in 2003 dollars. But they show that:

- Combined **rehab + behavioral therapies** account for a meaningful chunk of per‑child spending, especially in Medicaid.


### c) Systematic review of ASD healthcare costs

Karami Matin et al. (2022 systematic review, 37 studies) concluded that in individuals with ASD:[^5][^2]

- **Therapeutic interventions, outpatient visits, and medications** constitute the **largest share of direct medical expenditures**.
- In some studies, **behavioral and rehabilitation therapies** account for **over half** of direct medical costs:
    - Wang et al. study (not the same as above) – behavioral therapies **54.3%** of health expenditures.[^2]
    - Mosadeghrad et al. (Iran) – rehabilitation services **70%** of annual direct medical ASD costs.[^2]

These are not U.S. pediatric‑only breakdowns, but they support the pattern that **therapy services across disciplines dominate health spending for ASD**.

### Takeaway

- At the system level (MEPS), **20k dollars/year** per treated ASD child, of which about **10k is ambulatory care** likely encompassing multiple therapy types.[^4]
- In specific claims datasets, **therapy categories (OT/PT, speech, behavior therapy)** add up to **hundreds to thousands of dollars per child per year**, depending on payer and context.[^6][^2][^1]
- There is **no single U.S. dataset** that publishes “total combined *therapy* spending across all disciplines per ASD child per year,” but combining MEPS and claims studies you can see that therapy (all types) is a **major component of the ~20k/year total**.

***

## 3. How often ASD children receive 2+ disciplines at the same provider vs different providers

No claims‑based or survey‑based study was found that quantifies **site of care overlap** like:

> “X% of ASD children get ABA + OT + SLP at the same clinic vs in separate agencies.”

Limitations:

- Claims datasets (Medicaid MAX, MarketScan, MEPS) can, in principle, be used to identify NPI or TIN overlaps across disciplines, but **no published analysis seems to have done this**.
- Integrated health‑system studies (e.g., Kaiser Permanente work on ASD service use) distinguish **within‑system vs outside‑system** care but **do not break out** whether multiple disciplines are housed in the same clinic vs spread across independent providers.

What is available instead is **trend‑level** information:

- CentralReach’s Autism \& IDD Care Market Reports (proprietary CanaryBI dataset) consistently report strong growth in **multidisciplinary services (ABA + OT + SLP, etc.)**:
    - Multidisciplinary services show about a **30% compound annual growth rate (CAGR) since 2020**.[^7][^8]
    - “Multidisciplinary services have grown **56% since 2021**” in volume across ~1,000 therapy providers in their dataset.[^9]

These show that **more providers are offering multiple disciplines under one roof**, but they do **not** tell you, for a given child, how often all therapies are co‑located vs fragmented.

So for this bullet, the honest answer is: **no published quantitative breakdown exists**.

***

## 4. Revenue data from multidisciplinary pediatric ASD‑focused therapy practices

Here the evidence is almost entirely **absent** from peer‑reviewed or large survey sources.

What exists:

- Claims and survey studies report **healthcare expenditures per ASD child** (payer + sometimes out‑of‑pocket), not **clinic revenue**. Examples:
    - Wang et al. (Medicaid vs private): 22,653 vs 5,254 dollars per ASD child per year.[^1]
    - MEPS: 20,122 dollars/year for treated ASD children across all providers.[^4]
    - Multiple cost studies summarized by Karami Matin et al. show annual medical costs in the **4‑15k USD** range in various countries, often higher when including behavioral interventions.[^5][^2]
- Business brokers and M\&A write‑ups sometimes list **total annual revenue** for autism treatment centers or multidisciplinary pediatric clinics, but:
    - They do not disclose patient counts or case mix.
    - They are not claims‑based or methodologically documented.
    - They are effectively **anecdotal** and fall outside the “peer‑reviewed, survey‑based” standard you specified.

There is **no credible claims‑ or survey‑based source** that reports, for example:

> “A typical multidisciplinary ASD clinic generates X dollars of revenue per patient per year from ABA + OT + SLP + psych.”

Any such figures have to be **modeled** using:

- Per‑discipline utilization (hours/visits/year),
- Fee schedules and contracts,
- Clinic‑level payer mix.

***

## 5. Data from CentralReach, Rethink, or similar platforms on cross‑discipline utilization

The publicly available information from **CentralReach** and similar autism/IDD practice platforms focuses on **market trends**, not raw utilization distributions.

From CentralReach Autism \& IDD Care Market Reports:[^10][^8][^7][^9]

- The reports are based on **CanaryBI**, described as the largest autism/IDD dataset, including:
    - Claims
    - Insurance payments
    - Service hours
    - Providers and clients across ABA, OT, SLP, and related services.[^7][^10][^9]

Key published findings:

- **Demand growth:** Autism services grew **49% year‑over‑year** between Q1–Q3 2023.[^9]
- **Multidisciplinary growth:**
    - Shift toward integrated multidisciplinary services under one provider.
    - Multidisciplinary services have a roughly **30% CAGR since 2020** and have **grown 56% since 2021** in their dataset.[^8][^7][^9]
- **Operational benchmarks:** caregiver‑training utilization is associated with ~35.8% lower client churn; workforce turnover is high but improving in top decile providers.[^7][^9]

However:

- CentralReach does **not** publicly release:
    - Average number of disciplines per client,
    - Percentage of clients receiving ABA + OT + SLP concurrently,
    - Hours per discipline per ASD child.

Rethink, CR Essentials, and other ASD‑focused platforms similarly emphasize:

- Workflow, outcomes, and compliance features,
- Case studies and marketing metrics,

but **no public, claims‑level cross‑discipline utilization data**.

So:

- The platforms **clearly have** the data you care about.
- None of them have **published** cross‑discipline utilization statistics in the detailed, per‑child way you are asking for.

***

## Overall synthesis

Using only claims‑ and survey‑based sources:

- **Concurrency of disciplines:**
    - Claims show that ASD children often receive **multiple therapy categories in the same year** (speech, OT/PT, behavioral), with higher use in Medicaid than private insurance.[^6][^1]
    - There is **no published statistic** for “average number of therapy disciplines concurrently,” nor for **co‑location** (same vs different providers).
- **Combined therapy spending per ASD child per year:**
    - System‑level: ASD children average **~20k USD/year total health expenditures**, with **~10k USD in ambulatory care**, which includes therapies.[^4]
    - Claims‑level: OT/PT, speech, and behavior therapy account for **hundreds to several thousand dollars/year per child**, and multiple cost studies show therapy and behavioral interventions are often the **largest or dominant share** of direct medical ASD costs.[^5][^2][^1]
- **Same‑clinic vs multi‑provider patterns:**
    - No claims‑based publications quantify what share of ASD children get 2+ disciplines within the same organization vs fragmented across agencies.
- **Clinic revenue:**
    - There is **no claims/survey source** that reports revenue per ASD child per year specifically for **multidisciplinary pediatric therapy practices**.
- **Vendor/platform data (CentralReach, etc.):**
    - Public reports confirm **rapid growth of multidisciplinary services** and provide high‑level benchmarks (e.g., 30% CAGR, 56% growth since 2021).[^8][^9][^7]
    - But they do not publish **per‑child cross‑discipline utilization metrics**.

If you need precise numbers (e.g., “median ASD child uses 2 disciplines concurrently, 40% use 3+”), the only route is **original analysis of a large claims dataset (e.g., Medicaid, commercial claims) or direct collaboration with a platform like CentralReach**. The published literature currently does not provide that granularity.
