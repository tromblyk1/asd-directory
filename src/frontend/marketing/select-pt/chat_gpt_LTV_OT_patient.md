Occupational therapy (OT) is similar to PT structurally, but there are some key differences depending on setting.

Let’s break it down by **outpatient adult ortho/neuro OT**, since that’s the closest apples-to-apples comparison to PT.

Typical outpatient OT episode:

• 8–16 visits
• Reimbursement roughly $95–$160 per visit (similar billing codes, similar payer constraints)

So:

Low end:
8 × $95 ≈ **$760**

Mid range:
12 × $120 ≈ **$1,440**

Higher complexity case:
16 × $150 ≈ **$2,400**

So a typical outpatient OT episode usually lands around **$1,200–$2,000** in revenue.

Now here’s where OT can differ from PT in interesting ways:

1. Neuro and hand therapy cases often run longer.
2. Pediatric OT (especially autism, sensory integration, feeding therapy) can be much longer duration.
3. Certain developmental cases can be semi-long-term.

If you look at pediatric OT:

• 1–2 visits per week
• Often 6–12 months
• Reimbursement often $100–$180 per visit depending on state and payer

Let’s do conservative math:

1 visit/week × 40 weeks × $120
= **$4,800**

Twice per week for a year:
2 × 40 × $120
= **$9,600**

That’s a completely different LTV profile.

In home health OT, the reimbursement model changes (per episode bundles), but total per episode revenue often lands in the **$1,500–$3,000** range.

So quick summary:

Adult outpatient OT LTV per episode:
≈ **$1,200–$2,000**

Pediatric developmental OT annual LTV:
≈ **$4,000–$10,000+**

Lifetime across multiple years (common in developmental conditions):
Can realistically reach **$10k–$30k+ per child** over time.

That’s not because anyone is gaming anything. It’s just that developmental needs don’t resolve in 8 visits like a sprained ankle.

Now zoom out.

If you’re looking at this through an entrepreneurial lens — which you usually are — OT has one big structural advantage over PT:
Chronic pediatric demand creates higher cumulative LTV if payer access and authorization pipelines are solid.

But the tradeoff?
Heavy documentation burden, insurance auth headaches, and staff burnout risk.

Bottom line:

• Adult OT ≈ similar to PT: **$1k–$2k per episode**
• Pediatric OT can be dramatically higher: **$5k–$10k+ per year per child**
• True lifetime value depends on recurrence, retention, and payer mix

Healthcare revenue is rarely about one transaction. It’s about duration, compliance, and systems.

The real leverage is in operations, not in squeezing a CPT code.
