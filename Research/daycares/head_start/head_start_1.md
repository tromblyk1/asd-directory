# I'm looking for Head Start and Early Head Start programs in Florida that mention serving children with disabilities or autism on their websites. Give me specific program names, cities, counties, websites, and what disability language they use. Just list what you find, skip the disclaimers.

Here are Florida Head Start / Early Head Start programs whose own materials explicitly mention serving children with disabilities, special needs, or autism.

***

## Hillsborough County

### Hillsborough County Head Start / Early Head Start (County‑run program)

- **City / County:** Multiple sites across Hillsborough County (Tampa area)[^1][^2]
- **Website:** https://hcfl.gov/residents/children-and-families/education-and-schools/head-start-early-head-start[^1]
- **What they say:**
    - “The Head Start program is available for children from low‑income families between up to 5 years old, **including those with special needs**. Early Head Start is for children up to 3 years old; Head Start is for children 3‑5 years old.”[^1]

*(Their detailed brochure also notes that children with disabilities and their families receive a full range of comprehensive services.)*[^2]

## Statewide / Multi‑County Grantees

### Community Coordinated Care for Children, Inc. (4C) – Head Start / Early Head Start (Osceola \& Seminole; EHS in Orange, Osceola, Seminole)

- **Regions:**
    - Head Start: Osceola and Seminole counties
    - Early Head Start: Orange, Osceola, and Seminole counties[^3]
- **Website:** https://4cflorida.org/families/early-head-start-and-head-start-ehs-hs/[^3]
- **What they say:**
    - “Head Start is a federally funded program for low‑income children and **children with disabilities**, with an emphasis on cognitive, social, emotional, physical and mental development for the child.”[^3]

***

### Episcopal Children’s Services – Head Start / Early Head Start (Northeast \& North‑Central Florida)

- **Regions:** Multiple counties in Northeast and North‑Central Florida (includes Head Start and Early Head Start centers)[^4]
- **Website:** https://www.ecs4kids.org/programs/head-start/[^4]
- **What they say:**
    - “The Early Head Start Program provides full‑day, year‑round services to pregnant women and families of infants and toddlers, **including children with disabilities**.”[^4]
    - “Head Start… serves preschool‑age children and **children with disabilities**. All children receive developmental screenings…”[^4]

***

### Lutheran Services Florida (LSF) Head Start

- **Regions:** Multiple Florida counties (Panhandle, Tampa Bay, Southwest, etc.)[^5]
- **Website:** https://www.lsfheadstart.org[^5]
- **What they say (FAQ / program text):**
    - “Head Start is a comprehensive early childhood development program that serves income‑eligible children and their families.”[^5]
    - In their FAQ, they address: “Do Head Start programs accept **children with disabilities or special needs?**” and explain that children with disabilities are eligible and served within Head Start.[^5]

***

### Florida Head Start Association – statewide description (applies to all FL Head Start grantees)

- **Website:** https://www.flheadstart.org/hs[^6]
- **What they say:**
    - “Our center‑based programs are designed to foster the development of children, **including children experiencing homelessness and disabilities**, in a safe, nurturing environment.”[^6]

***

### Florida Head Start / Early Head Start – FL Department of Education overview

- **Website:** https://www.fldoe.org/schools/early-learning/parents/head-start.stml[^7]
- **What they say:**
    - “Florida’s Head Start and Early Head Start programs are available through school districts, private providers, home‑based programs, faith‑based agencies, family child care homes, capital action agencies and other locally developed programs.”[^7]
    - These programs “provide intensive comprehensive child development and family support services” to eligible children, and, as clarified in federal/advocacy documents, they must reserve at least **10% of enrollment for children with disabilities**.[^8]

***

### Head Start – Children with Disabilities (federal Head Start guidance, used by Florida programs)

- **Website:** https://headstart.gov/children-disabilities[^9]
- **What they say:**
    - “Individualizing for children who need more support helps ensure effective teaching for **children with disabilities and other special needs** across all the Head Start Early Learning Outcomes Framework domains.”[^9]

***

### “Florida Head Start” description via UCF‑CARD (used by FL programs)

- **Website:** https://ucf-card.org/about-asd/asd-ages-5-18/school/[^10]
- **What they say:**
    - “**Florida Head Start**, Head Start is a comprehensive preschool program for 3 to 5 year olds at risk for school failure due to income or environmental conditions. Up to **10% of Head Start openings may be used by children with disabilities**, regardless of their family’s income.”[^10]

### Easterseals Florida – Head Start Centers (North County)

- **Region:** “North County” (Easterseals South Florida – Head Start centers, referenced as serving children with autism)[^11]
- **Website:** (Directory profile) https://beaminghealth.com/providers/easter-seals-florida-head-start-centers-north-county-miami-gardens-fl[^11]
- **What they say (about their Head Start centers):**

### Community Action / Hope Florida listing – Early Head Start / Head Start
