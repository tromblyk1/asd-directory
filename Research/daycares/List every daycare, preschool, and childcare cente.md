## Important caveats

- This is **not** exhaustive; many inclusive centers either do not document autism supports explicitly online or are buried in local listings.
- You should verify: current enrollment ages, waitlists, whether they accept children with higher-support needs, and that they are not primarily ABA/PPEC and still operating under the same model.
- I’ve focused on centers that look like early education/childcare or inclusive preschools, and skipped pure K–12 special-needs schools unless they clearly function as early-childhood care.

## Why this cannot be a complete statewide list

- No public index: Florida does not maintain a statewide list of childcare centers that explicitly mention autism/ASD or “sensory-friendly” on their websites, and commercial childcare directories do not filter at this level of detail.[^6][^7]
- Marketing language is inconsistent: Many centers use broad “special needs” or “inclusive” wording without naming autism or sensory accommodations, or they rely on district ESE supports rather than preschool marketing.[^8]
- Many supports are informal/offline: Sensory rooms, visual schedules, or autism-trained staff may be present but never listed on public pages, especially in smaller centers or faith-based programs.[^7]

To reach something closer to “every” qualifying site, you’d likely need:

- A custom crawler seeded with Florida DCF-licensed childcare facilities,
